# **App Name**: ServiceBay

## Core Features:

- Repair Order Creation: First officers can create detailed repair orders including vehicle mileage, priority levels, estimated completion time, initial checklists, and additional comments.
- Intelligent Checklist & Category Suggestion: An AI-powered tool suggests relevant repair categories and checklist items based on the initial problem description provided in the order form, streamlining the creation process.
- Workshop Order Queue: Workshop officers can view an organized list of pending repair orders, prioritized by urgency and expected time, facilitating efficient task management.
- Bay & Technician Assignment: Officers can assign vehicles to one of the 9 available repair bays or designate for 'outside repair', and subsequently assign a technician after initial vehicle check, along with a proposed time.
- Repair Status Management: Track the progression of each repair order through clear status changes: 'Pending' -> 'In Progress' -> 'Completed'.
- Repair Completion Details: Upon job completion, officers record the performing technician, specific repair categories (e.g., Engine System, Brake), and any final comments.
- Reporting & Analytics: Generate reports summarizing repair activities, technician performance, and average completion times to inform operational decisions.

## Style Guidelines:

- The primary color is a deep, professional blue (#2952A3), conveying reliability and efficiency. This will be used for interactive elements, headlines, and key informational accents.
- The background color is an almost-white light blue-gray (#F9FAFB), providing a clean, spacious canvas that enhances readability for forms and data tables.
- A vibrant cyan (#13C9EC) serves as the accent color, drawing attention to calls-to-action, active states, and critical alerts with its high contrast and energy.
- The typeface 'Inter' (sans-serif) is chosen for its clear legibility across all text sizes and its modern, neutral aesthetic, suitable for both headlines and detailed body text within a management system.
- Employ a consistent set of line-art icons that are clear, minimalist, and functionally intuitive. Icons should be easily recognizable for actions like 'assign', 'edit', 'complete', and various repair categories.
- Emphasize a clean, organized layout with ample white space. Dashboards should feature a modular design, forms should be logically grouped with clear labels, and tabular data should be easily scannable and filterable.
- Implement subtle and functional animations, such as smooth transitions between states (e.g., when updating an order status) and clear loading indicators, to enhance user experience without distraction.
